Vocal — The Core of Classical Music

Why We Teach Vocal First

All instruments in Hindustani music were invented to imitate the human voice. Learning vocal first makes you a better musician on every instrument.

The Original Instrument

The sitar, sarangi, and bansuri all try to reproduce what the human voice does naturally — glides, ornaments, and microtonal nuances. Vocal training gives you the deepest understanding of how Hindustani music works.

Builds Your Ear First

Before you can play a raag, you need to hear it. Vocal training develops your ear for pitch and rhythm faster than any instrument. Students who sing first learn instruments twice as quickly afterward.

No Equipment to Start

You can begin today with nothing more than your breath and a Tanpura app on your phone. No instrument to buy, no setup, no waiting. The voice is always with you.

Khayal, Thumri, Bhajan

Our vocal curriculum covers the three main styles — Khayal (the core classical form), Thumri (expressive semi-classical), and Bhajan (devotional). Each has its own grammar, mood, and technique.

Examination Preparation

We prepare students for Gandharva Mahavidyalaya, Prayag Sangeet Samiti, and Bhatkhande examinations — from Prarambhik to Visharad. Many of our students have ranked in merit lists.

All Ages Welcome

We teach children from age 5 and adults up to their 70s. Vocal cords do not have an age limit. The only requirement is regular practice and a willingness to listen carefully.

Instruments Taught

Beyond Vocal

We also offer dedicated instrument courses once you have a foundation in the basics of Hindustani music.

Harmonium

India's most common accompanying instrument. Learn scales, bandishes, and bhajan accompaniment from scratch.

Tabla

The heartbeat of Hindustani music. Teen Taal thekas to solo compositions. Taught by a Lucknow Gharana-trained Ustad.

Sitar

Meend, Gamak, and Mizrab technique from the beginning. Raag training integrated from the first month.

Bansuri

Bamboo flute. Breath technique, sur, and raag — a deeply meditative instrument suited to quiet, patient learners.
