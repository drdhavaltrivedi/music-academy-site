Raag Library — राग पुस्तकालय

Detailed profiles of 20+ raags — swaras, aaroh-avaroh, vadi-samvadi, time, season, and rasa

Raag Yaman
Kalyan Thaat
Evening · 6–9 PM
Aaroh (Ascent)
NiReGaMa↑DhaNiSa'
Avaroh (Descent)
Sa'NiDhaMa↑GaReSa
Vadi: Ga
Samvadi: Ni
Jati: Sampurna–Sampurna
Peaceful, devotional, romantic. The signature of all 7 Shuddha swaras plus Tivra Ma — a complete, harmonious raag ideal for beginners.
All seasons · Especially Monsoon
Raag Bhupali
Kalyan Thaat
Evening · 6–9 PM
Aaroh
SaReGaPaDhaSa'
Avaroh
Sa'DhaPaGaReSa
Vadi: Ga
Samvadi: Dha
Jati: Audav–Audav (pentatonic)
Serene, pure, devotional longing. Uses only 5 swaras (no Ma, no Ni), making it one of the easiest and most beautiful raags to learn.
Winter evenings
Raag Bhairav
Bhairav Thaat
Morning · Sunrise
Aaroh
SaRe♭GaMaPaDha♭NiSa'
Avaroh
Sa'NiDha♭PaMaGaRe♭Sa
Vadi: Dha♭
Samvadi: Re♭
Jati: Sampurna–Sampurna
Deeply meditative, solemn, spiritual. Associated with Shiva. The Komal Re and Komal Dha give it a gravitas suited to early morning sadhana.
Winter mornings
Raag Kafi
Kafi Thaat
Night · 9 PM–12 AM
Aaroh
SaReGa♭MaPaDhaNi♭Sa'
Avaroh
Sa'Ni♭DhaPaMaGa♭ReSa
Vadi: Pa
Samvadi: Sa
Jati: Sampurna–Sampurna
Romantic, longing, bittersweet. Widely used in thumri and folk music (Hori). Its Komal Ga gives a rustic, heartfelt quality. Very popular in Bollywood compositions.
Spring (Holi season)
Raag Todi
Todi Thaat
Morning · 9 AM–12 PM
Aaroh
SaRe♭Ga♭Ma↑PaDha♭NiSa'
Avaroh
Sa'NiDha♭PaMa↑Ga♭Re♭Sa
Vadi: Dha♭
Samvadi: Re♭
Jati: Sampurna–Sampurna
Serious, profound, contemplative. One of the most complex and revered raags — 3 Komal swaras and Tivra Ma create tension and deep introspection. Considered king of morning raags.
Winter
Raag Bageshri
Kafi Thaat
Night · 9 PM–12 AM
Aaroh
SaGa♭MaDhaNi♭Sa'
Avaroh
Sa'Ni♭DhaMaGa♭ReSa
Vadi: Ma
Samvadi: Sa
Jati: Audav–Shadav
Deep yearning, devotion, spiritual longing. Associated with Meera Bai's bhajans. The absence of Re in aaroh gives it a unique restlessness.
All seasons
Raag Darbari Kanada
Asavari Thaat
Deep Night · 12–3 AM
Aaroh
SaReGa♭MaPaDha♭Ni♭Sa'
Avaroh
Sa'Ni♭Dha♭PaMaGa♭ReSa
Vadi: Re
Samvadi: Pa
Jati: Sampurna–Sampurna
Royal, majestic, austere grandeur. Created by Miyan Tansen for Emperor Akbar's court. The characteristic andolan (oscillation) on Komal Ga is its hallmark. Considered one of the most powerful raags.
All seasons · Late night
Raag Bihag
Bilawal Thaat
Night · 9 PM–12 AM
Aaroh
SaGaMaMa↑PaNiSa'
Avaroh
Sa'NiDhaPaMaGaReSa
Vadi: Ga
Samvadi: Ni
Jati: Audav–Sampurna
Romantic, sweet, tender. Uses both Shuddha and Tivra Ma, giving it a playful, lively character. Very popular in film music — "Lag Ja Gale" is based on Bihag.
All seasons
Raag Marwa
Marwa Thaat
Evening · Dusk
Aaroh
SaRe♭GaMa↑DhaNiSa'
Avaroh
Sa'NiDhaMa↑GaRe♭Sa
Vadi: Re♭
Samvadi: Dha
Jati: Shadav–Shadav (no Pa)
Restless, anxious, unfulfilled longing. The absence of Pa and presence of both Komal Re and Tivra Ma creates a suspended, yearning quality unlike any other raag. Evokes dusk and the transition of day to night.
Winter evenings
Raag Bilawal
Bilawal Thaat
Morning · 6–9 AM
Aaroh
SaReGaMaPaDhaNiSa'
Avaroh
Sa'NiDhaPaMaGaReSa
Vadi: Dha
Samvadi: Ga
Jati: Sampurna–Sampurna
Cheerful, fresh, optimistic. All Shuddha swaras — equivalent to the Western major scale (C major). Represents the natural, joyful mood of a bright morning.
Spring
Puriya Dhanashri
Purvi Thaat
Evening · Sunset
Aaroh
SaRe♭GaMa↑PaDha♭NiSa'
Avaroh
Sa'NiDha♭PaMa↑GaRe♭Sa
Vadi: Pa
Samvadi: Sa
Jati: Sampurna–Sampurna
Majestic, serene devotion with grandeur. Often played at sunset — the Tivra Ma and Komal Re&Dha together create a cathedral-like spiritual atmosphere.
Autumn
Raag Malkauns
Bhairavi Thaat
Deep Night · 12–3 AM
Aaroh
SaGa♭MaDha♭Ni♭Sa'
Avaroh
Sa'Ni♭Dha♭MaGa♭Sa
Vadi: Ma
Samvadi: Sa
Jati: Audav–Audav (only 5 notes)
Dark, mysterious, fierce, and powerful. No Re, no Pa — only 5 notes, all dark. Legend says Tansen sang this to put out fire lit by Raag Deepak. Equivalent to the Western minor pentatonic scale.
All seasons · Late night

Understanding Raag Notation

Each raag profile uses standard Hindustani notation. Here is a quick reference guide.

Ga

Shuddha (natural) swara

Ga♭

Komal (flat) swara

Ma↑

Tivra (sharp) swara — only Ma

Sa'

Upper octave (taar saptak)
